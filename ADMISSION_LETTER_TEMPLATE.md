## SCHOOL POLICIES & EXPECTATIONS

All students admitted to Ibun Baz Girls Secondary School are expected to uphold the values, standards, and policies of the institution. These guidelines ensure a safe, respectful, and conducive learning environment.

### 1. DRESS CODE & UNIFORM REQUIREMENTS

**School Uniform (Mandatory):**
- **Regular School Days:** Navy blue skirt (knee-length, modest fit), white blouse (long-sleeved or short-sleeved with collar), navy blue school jacket, white socks, black closed-toe shoes
- **Physical Education:** Navy blue PE shorts, white PE shirt, white sports socks, sports shoes
- **Religious Days/Islamic Studies:** Modestly designed hijab (optional but encouraged), maintaining school colors where possible
- **Weekend/Casual Days:** Conservative, modest clothing (no transparent or revealing garments)

**Key Requirements:**
- Hair must be neatly groomed; covered with hijab is encouraged in line with Islamic teachings
- No makeup, nail polish, or visible jewelry except simple earrings or watches
- All attire must be clean and well-maintained
- Modifications to uniform are not permitted
- Uniform violations result in disciplinary action

**Rationale:** The uniform fosters school identity, discipline, and ensures a modest, focused learning environment in line with Islamic values.

---

### 2. ATTENDANCE & PUNCTUALITY

**Expected Standards:**
- **Reporting Time:** 6:45 AM (Assembly begins at 7:00 AM sharp)
- **Closing Time:** 4:30 PM (Monday—Friday)
- **Absenteeism:** Maximum 2 days unexplained absence per term; beyond this requires medical documentation
- **Lateness:** Arriving after 7:15 AM is considered late; 3 instances of lateness = 1 day absence

**Attendance Requirements:**
- Minimum 85% attendance required to sit for examinations
- Parents must provide written explanation for any absence exceeding 3 consecutive days
- Medical certificates required for absences due to illness (exceeding 2 days)
- Family emergencies must be communicated to the school within 24 hours

**Consequences of Poor Attendance:**
- Parents/guardians are contacted for unexplained absences
- Restricted participation in school activities
- Academic probation for students below 75% attendance
- Possible expulsion for chronic absenteeism (below 60%)

---

### 3. BEHAVIOR & DISCIPLINE GUIDELINES

**Expected Conduct:**
- Show respect to all staff, teachers, and fellow students
- Use courteous language; profanity or disrespectful speech is prohibited
- Maintain order in all school facilities and during assemblies
- Follow all instructions from teachers and school authorities
- Report any bullying, harassment, or misconduct to management immediately

**Prohibited Behaviors:**
- ❌ Bullying, harassment, or intimidation of any student (consequences: suspension/expulsion)
- ❌ Theft, dishonesty, or cheating (consequences: expulsion)
- ❌ Violence, fighting, or aggressive behavior (consequences: immediate suspension + counseling)
- ❌ Bringing weapons, drugs, alcohol, or banned substances (consequences: expulsion + legal action)
- ❌ Inappropriate relationships or conduct (consequences: expulsion)
- ❌ Disrespect to teachers or school authority (consequences: detention + parent meeting)
- ❌ Vandalism or damage to school property (consequences: financial restitution + suspension)
- ❌ Unauthorized absence from lessons (consequences: detention + parent notification)

**Disciplinary Measures (Progressive):**
1. **Verbal Warning** - First minor infraction
2. **Written Warning** - Continued misbehavior
3. **Detention** - After-school detention for 1-3 hours
4. **Parent Notification** - Parent/guardian meeting required
5. **Suspension** - 1-14 days for serious violations
6. **Expulsion** - For severe, repeated, or criminal offenses

**School Discipline Committee:** Reviews all serious cases and makes final decisions. Parents have the right to appeal within 7 days.

---

### 4. RELIGIOUS & PRAYER OBLIGATIONS

**Islamic Framework:**
Ibun Baz Girls Secondary School is a faith-based institution committed to integrating Islamic teachings throughout the curriculum and school life.

**Prayer Requirements:**
- **Dhuhr Prayer (Midday):** Mandatory for all Muslim students; non-Muslim students engage in quiet reflection
- **Prayer Times:** Prayers conducted in the designated prayer hall (separate facilities for female students)
- **Friday Khutbah & Jumu'ah:** Participation encouraged; special assembly held on Fridays
- **Prayer Attire:** Clean, modest clothing; hijabs provided in prayer hall if needed

**Religious Observance:**
- The school respects the Islamic calendar; holidays include Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, and Islamic New Year
- During Ramadan, the school provides support for fasting students; lighter meals at lunch, flexible PE activities
- Quranic recitation and Islamic studies are core curriculum components
- All students, regardless of religion, are taught Islamic values: honesty, community, respect, compassion

**Non-Muslim Students:**
- Fully respected and accommodated
- May opt out of prayer activities but encouraged to attend Islamic studies classes
- Alternative reflection time provided during prayer periods
- Dress code standards still apply; same high standards for all

---

### 5. ACADEMIC INTEGRITY & HOMEWORK EXPECTATIONS

**Learning Standards:**
- Students are expected to attend all classes and actively participate in learning
- Regular homework and assignments must be completed on time
- Copying another student's work or submitting work not your own constitutes cheating
- All examinations and tests must be done independently without assistance

**Cheating & Academic Dishonesty:**
- **First Offense:** Zero marks for that assignment/test + parent notification + detention
- **Second Offense:** Suspension + review of student's continuation at school
- **Third Offense:** Expulsion from the school

**Homework Guidelines:**
- Homework is set to consolidate classroom learning
- Students in S.1-S.2: Average 1.5-2 hours per day
- Students in S.3-S.4: Average 2.5-3.5 hours per day
- Parents should provide a quiet study space and monitor completion
- Late homework receives reduced marks (10% deduction per day late)

**Support Services:**
- Remedial classes available for struggling students
- Study groups and peer tutoring encouraged
- Counselors available to discuss academic challenges

---

### 6. HEALTH, SAFETY & EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

**Health & Wellness:**
- **Medical Room:** Staffed during school hours; trained first aiders available
- **Immunizations:** All students must be up-to-date on vaccinations
- **Illness:** Students who become ill during the day must report to the medical room; parents contacted if serious
- **Medication:** Any medication required must be kept in the medical room with parental authorization
- **Mental Health:** Counselors available; confidentiality maintained

**Safety Protocols:**
- Fire drills conducted monthly; all students must participate
- Emergency exits clearly marked; at least two exits per classroom
- Students must stay in designated areas during free periods
- No unauthorized persons allowed on school premises
- CCTV security cameras in public areas; camera-free in bathrooms and changing rooms
- School van monitored for safe transport

**Prohibited Items:**
- Sharp objects, lighters, matches, or flammable materials
- Mobile phones (to be kept at home; use in designated areas only)
- Valuables (school not responsible for lost items)
- Any items deemed dangerous or disruptive by management
